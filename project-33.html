Overview

Recent work at The Warehouse Group

Last couple years at TWG have been full of exciting projects ranging from UX to Service design. I’m currently in the process of updating my portfolio, so in the meantime sharing the summary of some key projects.

Delivery Service Initiative

Service and experience design

As part of my current role as Senior UX Designer, I’ve spent most of the last year building service design practice at The Warehouse Group (TWG). I’m leading a strategically important, omni-channel and group-wide project (TWG brands include Noel Leeming, Torpedo7, The Warehouse & Warehouse Stationery) as part of which I’m –

  • Helping identify & solve business problems through service design, while improving business processes.
  • Work with business on creating customer centric strategy.
  • Conduct cross functional workshops to identify priorities, ideate & test omni-channel experiences.
  • Solving omni-channel e2e journeys for an integrated & connected customer experience.
  • Plan all phases of design process and work through discovery to implementation to deliver the vision.

Torpedo7 Redesign

Lead UX

This was redesign project for the Torpedo7 e-commerce website. The goal was to build a responsive website to increase the mobile conversion, and also design a market leading store front for adventure gear. As a lead UX on this project, I worked with the business stakeholders, content & marketing stakeholders, product owner and UI designer to set a vision for the UX for the new e-commerce website. I worked on e2e design process from problem discovery, creating user flows, identifying jobs to be done for each step in the user journey and creating responsive wireframes. My responsibilities also included working with project manager on project timelines, doing design sprint planning, gathering requirements, collaborating and managing expectations with stakeholders, and facilitating sprint rituals. More than 6 months after the launch now, we’ve seen a substantial uplift in mobile conversions and a good baseline to keep improving the experience.

Noel Leeming delivery, collection & stock estimate

Since I joined TWG, I’m the lead UX on Noel Leeming along with working on some group-wide projects. The problem we’re facing last year was receiving lot of customer calls asking about deliveries, collection and store stock. And this was before they purchased a product. So we started with the objective of how might we improve online communication about delivery, Click & Collect and store stock estimate? I worked on e2e process for this project from discovery, ideate, prototype, rounds of user testing, UI design and worked with my remote product team to implement the final UI within agile delivery framework. All along the project we also ensured coaching the business and development team on customer-centric problem solving and design processes, as they were new to working closely with dedicated UX team. After delivering the solution, and enhancing the experience we successfully managed to reduce customer complaints around delivery, collection and store stock estimates.

Noel Leeming Services uptake

Noel Leeming offers a great range of services to help customers with their latest tech purchase. The objective for this project was to identify ways we could increase the online attach rate of services while purchasing products. I started the discovery by understanding user needs and journeys based on the Noel Leeming personas. The outcome of this project was a set of jobs or tasks users do at each part of their journey and ideas of how we can cater to those. These ideas, wireframes and discovery document has proven a useful piece of artefact to help inform Services strategy and roadmap, and coach the Services stakeholders on customer-centric design thinking process.

Noel Leeming Services IA

Following up on the services uptake discovery and ideation (mentioned above) as the first step we started redesigning the information architecture of how Noel Leeming Services were structured in the navigation. Being an important starting point for user discovery, it was crucial to create the right hierarchy to make the range of services visible to the users. We ran tree tests on Optimal Workshop to compare between 2 IA options to understand user behaviour and mental models. The best performaning IA was implemented on the website which resulted in an overall 113% growth in page views across all devices. The results were successful in increasing awareness and bringing visibility of our services range.
